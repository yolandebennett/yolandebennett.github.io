Marietta Duvalle Nelson

Portrait of Marietta Duvalle Nelson, c.1943

Marietta Duvalle Nelson (née White) was born in Medina, New York on October 13, 1919, to parents David White (a judge) and Mary Grace White.

From July 1943 to February 1944, Marietta served in Women Accepted for Voluntary Emergency Services (WAVES).

Marietta Duvalle Nelson and friend in WAVES uniform, 1943

WAVES was a military unit established during World War II as the U.S. Navy’s corps of female members.

Members served in a wide variety of capacities, ranging from performing essential clerical duties to serving as instructors for male pilots-in-training.
